# Linc GTM Foundation

This file provides stable go-to-market context for editorial classification. It tells the intelligence pipeline who Linc writes for, what frictions matter, what proof the market needs, and why each piece of content exists in the commercial motion.

This is not a content guide or a campaign brief. It changes rarely.

---

## Target segments

### cabinet-owner
Dirigeant ou associe d'un cabinet d'expertise comptable ou de paie. Decides on tools, pricing model, service quality, and migration risk. Cares about profitability, client retention, and operational calm. Evaluates Linc as a strategic choice, not a feature list.

### production-manager
Responsable de production paie au sein d'un cabinet. Manages a portfolio of client dossiers, controls quality, absorbs peak-period pressure. Cares about calculation clarity, control speed, DSN reliability, and support responsiveness. Evaluates Linc as a daily work tool.

### payroll-manager
Gestionnaire de paie in a cabinet or an enterprise. Produces bulletins, handles regulatory changes, runs controls, manages client questions. Cares about lisibilite du calcul, tracabilite, reduction of repetitive tasks, and fewer allers-retours. Evaluates Linc as the tool they open every morning.

### it-lead
Responsable SI or DSI in a cabinet or mid-size enterprise. Manages integration, data flows, migration planning, security. Cares about API quality, data portability, migration support, and operational risk. Evaluates Linc as a technical platform choice.

## Core market frictions

These are the real blockers and hesitations Linc content should address:

- Migration fear: switching payroll software mid-production is perceived as catastrophic risk
- Calculation opacity: incumbent tools produce correct numbers but no one can explain how
- Control burden: verification takes as long as production, especially on complex conventions
- Support degradation: large editors route support through layers that dilute expertise
- Pricing opacity: hidden costs, per-module billing, forced upgrades
- DSN anxiety: post-paie declarations carry compliance risk and are hard to debug
- Vendor lock-in: data is trapped, export is painful, switching cost is artificially high
- Convention coverage gaps: niche CCN support is incomplete, forcing manual workarounds

---

## Proof the market needs

What moves these buyers is not claims but evidence:

- Concrete calculation traces that show why a number is what it is
- Real migration timelines with actual dossier counts and transition steps
- Support response examples that demonstrate domain expertise, not ticket routing
- Side-by-side control workflows showing time saved on verification
- Convention-specific accuracy on edge cases incumbents handle poorly
- Client testimonials from cabinets of comparable size and complexity
- Transparent pricing comparisons without hidden modules

---

## Linc category beliefs

What Linc rejects and what it builds around:

- Payroll software should make calculations verifiable, not just automated
- A cabinet's time is best spent on control and client service, not on fighting the tool
- Support quality is a product feature, not a cost center to minimize
- Migration should be a planned operational transition, not a sales-driven cliff
- Pricing should be transparent and predictable, not a negotiation game
- The best product roadmap comes from field reality, not feature benchmarking
- Software should reduce charge mentale, not add new sources of it

## Content motions

### category
Content that shapes how the market thinks about payroll software. Targets unaware or problem-aware readers. Reframes the category around clarity, control, and cabinet reality. Not about Linc directly — about what the market should expect.

### demand-capture
Content that converts problem-aware or solution-aware readers into active evaluators. Shows concrete proof that Linc solves a friction they already feel. Evidence-heavy, specific, outcome-oriented.

### trust
Content that reinforces credibility with active buyers or existing clients. Shows operational depth, transparency, and alignment with cabinet values. Builds confidence in the team behind the product.

### recruiting
Content that attracts talent aligned with Linc's values and mission. Shows what it means to work on payroll software with this level of ambition and rigor.
