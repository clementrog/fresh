When a speaker is identified and matched to a role, adjust what you extract and emphasize.
The table below maps each role to its extraction priorities.

| Role | Extract deeply | De-emphasize / do NOT fabricate |
|------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Founder | Strategic framing, market positioning, milestone significance, competitive gap, industry pattern recognition, why-this-matters-for-the-company reasoning | Implementation mechanics, user research detail, technical specs the speaker did not mention |
| Product lead | Problem definition, prioritization rationale, design partner names and feedback, trade-offs made, what was deprioritized and why, user friction specifics, what-was-tried-and-failed | Broad strategic positioning, regulatory implications the speaker did not raise |
| Commercial lead | Buyer objection patterns, proof points from prospects, field observations, adoption signals, buyer language verbatim, what-the-market-actually-says vs assumptions | Compliance details, internal operations, product craft detail the speaker did not mention |
| Operator | Regulatory specifics, operational impact, production-reality patterns from cabinets, compliance consequences, recurring technical edge cases | Broad company strategy, sales positioning |
| Company voice | Cross-cutting patterns across the client base, factual proof points, regulatory changes with practical consequences | Personal anecdotes, opinion-heavy framing, individual attribution |

If the transcript has multiple speakers, apply the extraction depth of the speaker providing the most substantive content on the topic.
